## Worked example: "38yo woman, fatigue, irregular cycles, cold hands, 'normal labs'"

```markdown
## Quick read

This presentation spans thyroid, perimenopause-or-PCOS, and possibly nutrient insufficiency. "Normal labs" is the key phrase — TSH alone is often normal in early Hashimoto's, subclinical hypothyroidism, and euthyroid-sick syndrome. Priority: get her a complete workup before over-intervening with diet/supplements.

## Likely root-cause patterns to explore

1. **Thyroid dysfunction, incomplete workup** — cold hands + fatigue + irregular cycles is a classic thyroid triad. TSH-only testing misses early Hashimoto's and T4→T3 conversion problems. 🟩 evidence.
2. **PCOS or perimenopause** — depending on family history, weight trend, and specific cycle pattern. At 38, both are plausible.
3. **Iron/ferritin insufficiency** — common in menstruating women, often presents as fatigue before it shows up on CBC. 🟩 evidence.

## Coaching actions 🟢

**This session:**
- Explain why "normal labs" doesn't mean nothing is wrong — she deserves a fuller workup
- Start a 2-week symptom + cycle tracking journal (energy pattern, sleep, cycle details, digestion)
- Audit sleep: is she getting 7-9 hours? 10 PM–6 AM window?

**Nutrition foundations:**
- Protein at breakfast — aim 25-30g first thing (stabilizes blood sugar + cortisol). Well-evidenced for both thyroid and PCOS patterns.
- Selenium-rich foods daily: 2 Brazil nuts, or seafood 2x/week
- Iron-rich foods: red meat if eating meat, lentils + vitamin C source if plant-based

**Stress/sleep layer:**
- Nervous system regulation is upstream of both thyroid and hormones (cortisol suppresses T4→T3 conversion — 🟩 evidence). Even 5 minutes of diaphragmatic breathing morning/evening.

## For the client's clinician to request 🟡

**Labs worth asking for:**
- Full thyroid panel: TSH + Free T3 + Free T4 + TPO + TgAb (not just TSH)
- Ferritin + CBC + serum iron (fatigue workup)
- Fasting insulin + glucose (hidden insulin resistance)
- Vitamin D, B12
- If PCOS suspected: SHBG, DHEA-S, LH:FSH ratio, fasting insulin (pattern recognition for subtypes)

**Supplement considerations (education only, clinician prescribes):**
- If TPO antibodies positive → selenium has 🟨 evidence (2024 meta-analysis, 35 RCTs) for antibody reduction
- Vitamin A + D + K2 together at receptor-level (🟩 mechanism) — food sources first

## Referral triggers 🔴

- Goiter or neck swelling → endocrinology
- Rapid weight change (either direction) → medical workup
- Severe symptoms (palpitations, heat/cold intolerance extremes, tremor) → urgent
- If already on thyroid meds with no improvement → FM-trained physician for full reassessment

## Key questions for next session

- What's her morning energy like *before* coffee?
- Any family history of thyroid disease or autoimmune?
- What's her stress picture — work, caregiving, sleep debt?
- When did symptoms start? What was happening in her life 6 months before onset? (Timeline mapping — VitaOne's emotional-timeline framework)

## If she asks "should I take thyroid supplements?"

"Not until we know what we're working with. The good news is thyroid is very well-studied and a proper workup is straightforward. Let's get you the right labs first — I'll help you prepare for that conversation with your clinician, and we'll start with the foundations that support thyroid health regardless of what the labs show."

## Sources

- VitaOne posts: DXMUzjnDF6L (Perimenopause), DUA6B5dk_MK (Hairfall / thyroid T4→T3), DUavoWuEzzw (Autoimmune — for Hashimoto's context)
- Evidence: Thyroid §1 (🟩 — TSH alone insufficient), §2 (🟩 — cortisol suppresses conversion), §3 (🟩 — selenium/zinc/iron as cofactors), §4 (🟨 — selenium for antibodies)
- Research: [Huwiler 2024 Thyroid selenium meta-analysis](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38243784/)
```

## Notes on usage

- **Calibrate depth to the question.** If Shivani asks a quick "is selenium supported?", don't run the full scaffold — just give the evidence-check format.
- **Don't invent details.** If the source documents don't cover a claim, say so rather than fabricating.
- **Cultural relevance.** Default to Indian-context examples (methi, karela, amla, turmeric) alongside generic Western ones.
- **Scope > completeness.** Better to respond thoroughly in-scope than comprehensively in a way that blurs coaching and clinical roles.
