Steady Energy: Balancing Your Blood Sugar

A guide from Shivani Hari

That heavy, sleepy feeling after meals — and the afternoon crash — is often blood sugar swinging up then dropping. Smoothing those swings is one of the highest-return things you can do for energy, weight and long-term heart health.

The plate, simply: half non-starchy veg, a palm of protein (dal, paneer, curd, tofu), a thumb of healthy fat, and a smaller portion of grain — and eat them in that order where you can (veg/protein first, carbs last).

Swaps that work in an Indian kitchen:
- Millets (jowar, bajra, ragi) or dal-rotis in place of white rice/maida
- Whole fruit instead of juice
- Cut the sugar in chai; try cinnamon instead

The 10-minute trick: a gentle walk after lunch and dinner can lower the after-meal sugar spike by 20–30% — no gym needed.

Rhythm matters: aim for proper meals with gaps rather than constant grazing, and finish eating 2–3 hours before bed.

Small, steady changes here ripple out to better sleep, fewer cravings and a lighter, clearer feeling through the day.

This guide is for education and lifestyle support, not medical advice. Your personalised plan will give you the specifics. Always check with your doctor about medicines and lab results.
