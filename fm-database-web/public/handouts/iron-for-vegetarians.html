Building Your Iron — A Vegetarian Guide

A guide from Shivani Hari

Low iron stores (ferritin) are one of the most common — and most missed — reasons for tiredness, hair fall and brain fog, often before a blood count looks abnormal.

Why it's harder on a vegetarian plate: plant (non-haem) iron is absorbed less easily than iron from meat. The good news — a few simple habits change that a lot.

Eat more of: soaked-and-cooked dals (moong, masoor), dark leafy greens (palak, methi, amaranth), sesame (til), pumpkin seeds, ragi, jaggery in small amounts.

Absorption boosters:
- Pair iron foods with vitamin C — a squeeze of lemon, tomato, or amla at the same meal.
- Soak and sprout legumes; cook in a cast-iron kadhai.

Absorption blockers — separate these from iron meals by about an hour:
- Tea and coffee (the tannins block iron)
- Calcium-rich foods/supplements at the same time

If you take a thyroid tablet: keep iron at least 4 hours away from it — iron can stop the thyroid medicine working.

A note on tummy comfort: if an iron supplement makes you constipated, taking it every other day, with food, and with plenty of water and ground flax usually settles it.

This guide is for education and lifestyle support, not medical advice. Your personalised plan will give you the specifics. Always check with your doctor about medicines and lab results.
