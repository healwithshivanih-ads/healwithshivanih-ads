Kitchen Measures

A guide from Shivani Hari

Your plan uses everyday measures, not grams. Weigh a few of these against your
own bowls and spoons two or three times — your eye calibrates fast, and then you
can put the scale away. A little more or less is completely fine.

1 teaspoon (tsp) — 5 ml
- The small spoon you stir tea with.

1 tablespoon (tbsp) — 15 ml
- A heaped serving spoon — three teaspoons.
- Ghee / oil ≈ 14 g · chutney / pickle ≈ 18 g · seeds ≈ 10 g.

1 katori / small bowl — about 150 ml
- A standard steel katori.
- Dal ≈ 150 g · sabzi ≈ 120 g · curd ≈ 150 g.

1 bowl — about 250 ml
- A cereal or soup bowl, filled to a comfortable level.
- Cooked rice ≈ 200 g · dal or sabzi ≈ 200 g · salad ≈ 80 g.

1 cup — about 200 ml (we mean ~200 ml — not the 240 ml US cup)
- A regular tea or coffee cup, not a mug.
- Cooked rice ≈ 150 g · chopped veg ≈ 100 g · milk = 200 ml.

1 glass — about 200 ml
- A normal drinking glass — water, chaas, milk.

1 palm-sized portion — your protein serving
- As wide and thick as your own palm (fingers don't count).
- Paneer / tofu / fish / chicken ≈ 90–100 g cooked.

1 handful (mutthi) — what your cupped hand holds
- One cupped palm — your hand size is your portion.
- Nuts ≈ 25–30 g · puffed or dry snack ≈ 15 g.

1 piece — roti / dosa / chilla (the count in "(1)", "(2)"…)
- A roti about 6 inches across (≈ 30 g flour); a dosa from ≈ 30 g batter.

1 pinch (chutki) — a trace
- What you hold between thumb and one finger — for salt, spices, hing.

These are guides for the eye, not a lab spec. Eat to comfortable fullness and
trust your appetite.
