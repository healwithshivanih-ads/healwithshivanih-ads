Going Gluten-Free — What's Hiding in Your Food

A guide from Shivani Hari

Gluten is the protein in wheat, barley and rye. If we're trialling gluten-free together, the tricky part isn't the obvious foods — it's the surprising ones. Here's what to watch for.

The obvious ones (wheat-based)

  • Atta, maida, suji/rava (semolina), dalia, bran
  • Roti, chapati, naan, paratha, poori, bhatura
  • Bread, pasta, noodles, vermicelli (sevai), biscuits, rusk, cakes, pastries
  • Most namkeen, mathri, samosa, kachori (maida coating)

The not-so-obvious ones — these catch everyone out

  • Oats — naturally gluten-free, but almost always cross-contaminated with wheat in the field/mill. Use only certified gluten-free oats.
  • Hing (asafoetida) — most Indian "compounded" hing is bulked with wheat flour. Look for a gluten-free / wheat-free label (gluten-free hing exists).
  • Soy sauce — usually fermented with wheat. Choose tamari (gluten-free soy sauce) instead.
  • Malted drinks — Horlicks, Bournvita, Boost and most "malt" drinks contain barley malt = gluten.
  • Beer and malt vinegar (barley).
  • Ready gravies, soups, sauces, instant mixes — often thickened with maida or "wheat starch".
  • "Multigrain" or "atta" anything — multigrain almost always includes wheat.
  • Some masalas, pickles, papad, and asafoetida-based tadka — check the pack.
  • Imitation/mock meats (seitan) — seitan is pure wheat gluten.
  • Hidden on labels as: wheat, barley, rye, malt, semolina, hydrolysed wheat protein, modified starch (from wheat).

Naturally gluten-free — eat freely

  • Grains/flours: rice, jowar, bajra, ragi, foxtail & other millets, besan (gram), kuttu (buckwheat), rajgira (amaranth), singhara, makki (corn), quinoa
  • Everything fresh: all dals & legumes, vegetables, fruits, nuts, seeds
  • Dairy & eggs, plain (unflavoured)
  • Idli, dosa, plain dhokla (rice + lentil based — but check restaurant batters)

Three habits that make it easy

  1. Read the label for wheat / barley / rye / malt — every packaged item, every time.
  2. Swap the staples: wheat roti → jowar/bajra/ragi/besan roti; soy sauce → tamari; regular hing → gluten-free hing; malted drink → plain milk + a date.
  3. At restaurants/home, watch cross-contamination — a shared toaster, the same oil that fried pooris, a wheat-dusted tava.

A strict trial means truly strict for the agreed weeks — even small amounts keep the immune system stirred up, so the cleaner the trial, the clearer your answer.
