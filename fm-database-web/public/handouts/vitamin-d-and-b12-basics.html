Vitamin D & B12 — Quiet but Essential

A guide from Shivani Hari

Two nutrients that quietly affect energy, mood, immunity and nerves — and both are very common to run low on, especially on a vegetarian diet or with limited sun.

Vitamin D: made in the skin from sunlight, but sunscreen, indoor days and our latitude mean many of us sit below the ideal range. It supports immunity, mood, bone strength, blood sugar and thyroid. A little midday sun on the arms helps; most people also need a supplement to reach a good level — your plan will guide the amount, taken with a fatty meal (and usually paired with K2).

Vitamin B12: found mainly in animal foods, so vegetarians are often low even when blood looks 'normal-ish'. It powers energy, clear thinking, nerves and healthy blood. The active form (methylcobalamin) is well suited to vegetarian diets.

The plan: we top these up, then re-test after a couple of months to confirm you've reached a good range — not too low, not too high.

Small gaps here can quietly drag energy down; closing them often makes a noticeable difference within weeks.

This guide is for education and lifestyle support, not medical advice. Your personalised plan will give you the specifics. Always check with your doctor about medicines and lab results.
