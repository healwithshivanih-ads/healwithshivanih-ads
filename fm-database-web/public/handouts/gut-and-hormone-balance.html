Your Gut & Hormone Balance

A guide from Shivani Hari

Your gut does more than digest — it helps your body clear out used-up hormones. When digestion is sluggish or out of balance, hormones can recirculate instead of leaving, adding to heaviness, cycle troubles and bloating.

Keep things moving (this is the big one for hormone clearance):
- Fibre daily — ground flaxseed (1 tbsp), cooked veg, dals, fruit with the skin.
- Water through the day, and that post-meal walk.

Feed the good bugs: a variety of plants across the week, and gentle fermented foods introduced slowly (especially if certain foods give you a tight throat or flushing — go cautiously there).

Ease the load:
- Cruciferous veg (cabbage, cauliflower, broccoli) support the liver's hormone-clearing work.
- A warm cumin-coriander-fennel tea between meals can soothe bloating and acidity.

Be gentle with 'acidity': reaching for an antacid often masks the real driver (timing, trigger foods, stress, sometimes too little stomach acid). We look for the cause rather than just quieting the symptom.

When digestion settles into a daily rhythm, a lot of other things — energy, skin, cycles — tend to follow.

This guide is for education and lifestyle support, not medical advice. Your personalised plan will give you the specifics. Always check with your doctor about medicines and lab results.

— Shivani Hari · Functional Medicine Coaching
